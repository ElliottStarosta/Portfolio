Hey there, I'm Elliott !

I am a passionate 16-year-old software developer from Ottawa, Ontario. I enjoy creating innovative tools that solve real-world problems, making everyday tasks easier for everyone.

My coding journey started in grade 5 when I first printed "Hello, World!" in Python. From there, I kept pushing myself to build new things—like a GUI-based Tic-Tac-Toe game and a typewriter simulator—just to see how far I could take my ideas. Over time, I got hooked on the problem-solving side of coding, especially in back-end development. One of my favorite projects so far is the EOM Course Recommender, a Java-based application that helps high school students plan their courses using a machine learning algorithm. It was a fun challenge to create something that could actually help students make better academic decisions.

Beyond coding, I’m active in FRC (FIRST Robotics Competition), where I love tackling engineering challenges with my team. I also run my own hackathon club, organizing events for high school students to build and showcase their ideas. On top of that, I help tutor students in the Auxilium program, sharing my knowledge with others who are just getting started. At the end of the day, my goal is simple: build things that people will love and enjoy—even if they seem a little useless at first. Some of the best projects come from unexpected ideas, and I’m always looking for new ways to bring them to life!
